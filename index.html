About Bihar Tourism

Discover the rich cultural heritage, ancient universities, spiritual sites, and scenic beauty of Nalanda and Rajgir. These destinations attract visitors from all over the world. Bihar Tourism is renowned for its rich historical and cultural heritage, with Rajgir and Nalanda University being two of its most iconic destinations. Rajgir, surrounded by scenic hills, is famous for its ancient Buddhist sites, hot springs, and peaceful natural beauty. Nearby Nalanda is home to the ruins of the world-renowned ancient Nalanda University, a center of learning that attracted scholars from across Asia. Together, Rajgir and Nalanda offer visitors a unique journey through Bihar's glorious past, spiritual traditions, and architectural heritage.

Nalanda

Nalanda is famous for the ancient Nalanda University, one of the world's oldest centers of learning and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Main Attractions

  • Nalanda University Ruins

    • The ruins of Nalanda University in Bihar, India, stand as a testament to one of the world’s earliest and most renowned centers of learning, flourishing from the 5th to the 12th century CE.Nalanda University, also known as Nalanda Mahavihara, was founded around 427 CE by Emperor Kumaragupta I of the Gupta dynasty and expanded under subsequent rulers, including the Pala dynasty and Emperor Harsha . It operated for nearly 800 years, attracting scholars, monks, and students from across Asia, including China, Tibet, Korea, and Southeast Asia Nalanda was a major center for Buddhist learning, particularly Mahayana Buddhism, but also offered instruction in secular subjects such as grammar, logic, medicine, mathematics, astronomy, and the Vedas
  • Hiuen Tsang Memorial Hall
  • Nalanda Archaeological Museum
  • Surya Mandir

Rajgir

Rajgir is known for its historical significance, beautiful hills, hot springs, and Buddhist heritage.

Main Attractions

  • Vishwa Shanti Stupa
  • Rajgir Ropeway
  • Hot Water Springs
  • Griddhakuta Hill
  • Cyclopean Wall

Glass Bridge

The Rajgir Glass Bridge is one of Bihar’s most famous adventure attractions and is located inside the SUSPENSION BRIDGE NATURE SAFARI RAJGIR area in Rajgir. It was built to boost tourism and offers visitors the thrilling experience of walking on a transparent glass skywalk high above a valley. The glass skywalk is made of glass and steel and stands about 250 feet above the valley, offering spectacular views of the surrounding hills and forests. It is located amid the scenic hills of Rajgir, a city known for its rich Buddhist, Jain, and historical heritage.
